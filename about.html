ABOUT US

AOPA is proud to present the So Kids Can Move project in honor of the ADA and to promote inclusion for the patients we serve in all parts of society. We are initiating this state-based policy campaign to increase access to recreational prostheses as medically required healthcare for children in conjunction with the National Association for the Advancement of Orthotics & Prosthetics (NAAOP). For children who require proper, evidence-based prosthetic care in order to engage in physical activity, So Kids Can Move will increase access to the health and social advantages of physical activity.

In the beginning, Kids Can Move will concentrate on Oregon and Washington. We will work to have them join Maine as the first state to cover children's recreational prostheses.

We intend to expand this campaign to other states in order to see this same policy implemented after Oregon and Washington served as the pilot states for it. Through this initiative, AOPA's state-based policy work will be advanced, and more of our members will be able to supply and be compensated for recreational prosthesis for the kids you visit.
